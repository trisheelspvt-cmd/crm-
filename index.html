What is Customer Relationship Management (CRM)?

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) is a strategy and system used by organizations to manage interactions with current and potential customers. It focuses on improving relationships, increasing customer satisfaction, and driving business growth.

📌 About CRM

Customer Relationship Management (CRM) refers to the processes, tools, and technologies used by organizations to manage and analyze customer interactions throughout the customer lifecycle. The main goal is to improve customer relationships, enhance retention, and drive sales growth.

Key Components of CRM

Employees who interact with customers

🔄 Process

Steps followed to manage customer interactions

💻 Technology

CRM software and tools used to store and analyze data

🔄 Types of CRM

Operational CRM

Deals with daily operations like sales, marketing, and service. Example: handling customer inquiries, booking services.

Analytical CRM

Analyzes customer data to improve decision-making. Helps understand customer trends and preferences.

Collaborative CRM

Shares customer information across departments. Ensures better coordination between teams.
